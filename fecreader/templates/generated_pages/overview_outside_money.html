The numbers on this page refer to "independent expenditures", the most important class of spending by which outside groups and party committees get involved in federal elections. Most of the money comes from groups that aren't subject to contribution limits, and some do not report their donors at all. By law, these groups cannot "coordinate" with the candidate they are supporting or opposing, though those rules have been criticized as ineffectual.

Some groups, however, seem to report a single ad buy in two parts. The determination of whether a communication is positive or negative is solely up to the submitting group and is not audited.

Other PACs include traditional PACs which are subject to contribution limits.
